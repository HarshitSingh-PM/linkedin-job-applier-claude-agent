# Resume Tailoring Methodology

## Core Principles
- Maintain factual integrity while maximizing fit to the job description
- Never fabricate experience, but intelligently reframe and emphasize relevant aspects
- Aim for 90+ ATS score on a scale of 100
- Write text that is engaging, personal, and human-sounding. Avoid AI-sounding phrases.

## Resume Structure

### Subtitle / Headline
The subtitle MUST use the candidate's actual current job title - NOT the target role title. After the title, add 2-3 pipe-separated keywords from the JD.
Example: "Senior Product Manager | E-Commerce & Payments | P&L Ownership"

### Summary (3-4 lines max)
Write a human, personal, engaging summary. NO robotic phrasing.

Rules:
- Start with actual title and years of experience
- Include 1-2 concrete metrics naturally woven in
- End with what excites them about the domain
- AVOID: skill dumps, "strong track record", "proven ability", generic phrases
- Use simple, direct language. Short sentences.

### Experience Bullets
Every bullet MUST follow: **[Strong Action Verb] + [What you did] + [How] + [Quantified Result]**

- Bold the key metric: `**bold metric**`
- Lead with the most impressive metric
- Never use weak verbs: "Helped", "Assisted", "Worked on", "Responsible for"
- USE strong verbs: "Shipped", "Launched", "Built", "Drove", "Scaled", "Orchestrated"

### Skills Section
- Group into 4 categories that mirror JD requirements
- Max 15-20 items total
- Remove tools not relevant to target role
- Add JD-required tools if candidate has them

### Resume Length
- HARD RULE: Exactly 2 pages. Never 1, never 3.
- Adjust bullet counts to fit

## JD-Specific Tailoring
1. Naturally incorporate JD keywords into bullets where truthful
2. Put most JD-relevant experience first
3. Ensure skills section mirrors JD requirements
4. Emphasize bullets matching the archetype the JD seeks
5. Highlight same-industry experience

## Strong Action Verbs
Shipped, Launched, Built, Drove, Scaled, Orchestrated, Architected, Negotiated, Diagnosed, Prototyped, Pioneered, Secured, Discovered, Validated, Experimented, Monetized, Automated, Accelerated, Transformed, Revamped, Defined, Established, Executed, Delivered, Spearheaded

## IMPORTANT
1. NEVER fabricate metrics. Use real numbers from profile.yaml or add [X%] placeholders.
2. Preserve the candidate's authentic voice and career trajectory.
3. NO unicode characters in output text - no em dashes, curly quotes, arrows. ASCII only.
